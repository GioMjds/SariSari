# Character-Driven Scenes for SariSari Campaigns

To make the **SariSari** app highly relatable and culturally authentic, we use character-driven storytelling in our marketing. This document details our core character archetypes and the advertising scenes they participate in.

---

## Character Archetypes

### 1. Ate Sarah (The Shop Owner / _Tindera_)

- **Profile:** 34 years old, energetic, practical, and tech-savvy. She loves her store but gets overwhelmed during the afternoon rush. She wants to grow her business to send her kids to college.
- **Visuals:** Modern casual Filipina attire (e.g., hair tied up neatly, apron with pockets, friendly smile, always holds a smartphone).

### 2. Aling Nena (The Traditional _Suki_)

- **Profile:** 58 years old, regular neighborhood customer, grandmotherly but strict about her budget. She often asks to buy on credit (_utang_) and pays when her pension or salary arrives.
- **Visuals:** Traditional duster dress (_duster_), carrying a small change purse, well-known in the community.

### 3. Kuya Jun (The Supplier / Helper)

- **Profile:** 40 years old, delivery truck driver/supplier. He is always in a hurry to deliver cases of soft drinks and snacks, needing fast inventory checks before offloading cargo.
- **Visuals:** Wearing a dry-fit t-shirt, hand truck in tow, friendly and hard-working.

---

## Storytelling Scenes

### Scene 1: The Afternoon Rush (Sales POS)

- **Narrative:**
  School has just let out. Three kids are shouting their orders for snacks, Aling Nena wants her daily cooking oil pack, and Ate Sarah is at the center of it all.
- **Visual Concept:**
  - Split screen or wide illustration.
  - **Left side:** A chaotic counter with old-school calculators and pencils. Ate Sarah looks stressed, scratching her head.
  - **Right side (SariSari app):** Ate Sarah tap-tapping her phone with a smile. The screen shows a clean "Change computed: ₱23" message. The kids get their snacks instantly.
- **Key Message:** _"Mabilis na POS para sa matinding dagsa ng suki."_ (Fast POS for peak hours.)

### Scene 2: The Suki Balance Check (Utang Ledger)

- **Narrative:**
  Aling Nena comes to pay her balance but has lost her own little notebook where she writes down what she owes. She's worried Ate Sarah's calculations won't match hers.
- **Visual Concept:**
  - Aling Nena is looking nervous at the counter. Ate Sarah is showing her the smartphone screen.
  - The phone displays the SariSari app showing Aling Nena's profile: `₱450.00 Outstanding`, with each dated purchase clearly listed. Aling Nena looks relieved, smiling and nodding.
- **Key Message:** _"Walang hulaan, walang away. Malinaw na listahan ng utang na may tiwala."_ (No guessing, no arguments. Clear and trusted credit records.)

### Scene 3: The Supplier's Arrival (Inventory & Stock)

- **Narrative:**
  Kuya Jun's delivery truck pulls up. He asks Ate Sarah: "Ate, ilang case ng soft drinks at noodles ang kailangan mo ngayon?" In the past, Ate Sarah would have to walk around the shelves counting manually.
- **Visual Concept:**
  - Ate Sarah stands at the door, holding her phone.
  - The screen shows the SariSari Low Stock Alerts page indicating exactly 3 items remaining for _Brand X Noodles_ and 1 case remaining for _Coke_.
  - Kuya Jun is nodding in approval, ready to unload exactly what is needed.
- **Key Message:** _"Hindi mauubusan ng paninda. Isang tingin lang, alam mo na ang kulang."_ (Never run out of stock. One look and you know what's low.)

### Scene 4: The Brownout Test (Offline-First Invariant)

- **Narrative:**
  A sudden tropical storm hits the barangay, causing a power outage. Other shops nearby shut down or struggle with dark counters and no internet/data. Ate Sarah's store is dimly lit by a candle, but business goes on as usual.
- **Visual Concept:**
  - A cozy store interior during a brownout. Rain outside.
  - Ate Sarah continues to tap and record transactions on her phone, which operates completely offline. A small battery icon and "100% Offline Active" badge are highlighted on the screen.
- **Key Message:** _"Walang kuryente? Walang signal? Tuloy ang negosyo gamit ang SariSari."_ (No power? No signal? Business continues with SariSari.)
